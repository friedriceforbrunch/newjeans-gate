Misinformation & Conspiracies

"ILLIT is a copy of NewJeans"

Addresses allegations regarding Concept, Lore, Styling, Choreography, Sound, Marketing, and specific visual comparisons.

Concept: ILLIT's "Real Me" vs "Super Me" duality and lore-driven approach differs from NewJeans' nostalgic, persona-based concepts.

Styling: ILLIT's girlcore/coquette/Japanese fashion aesthetic contrasts with NewJeans' Y2K/90s streetwear.

Choreography: Statistical analysis and style comparison show minimal overlap and distinct approaches.

Sound: ILLIT's electronic/dream pop/hyper pop sound with video game elements differs from NewJeans' alternative R&B.

Marketing: ILLIT's survival show origin and pre-debut activities led to different marketing opportunities (e.g., PFW invitation) compared to NewJeans' concealed debut strategy.

Specific Allegations: Addresses claims about fonts, album covers/stickers, fashion show attendance, 3D billboards, Super ELLE, hanbok photoshoots, and commercial films, providing counter-evidence and context.

"ILLIT was created to replace NewJeans"

Examines the origin of this claim from a leaked monitoring report excerpt ("Replace New") and clarifies ADOR's explanation that it referred to IVE and was about LE SSERAFIM establishing its own domain, not replacing NewJeans with ILLIT. Notes the timeline of ILLIT's planning predates the report and their public name announcement.

"ILLIT plagiarized Plz Project"

Addresses the allegation that ILLIT's "Real Me" concept photos copied Plz Project. Notes Serian Heu's history of working with fashion brands, Plz Project's lack of public accusation despite brigading, and ILLIT wearing the brand's clothing elsewhere.

"ILLIT Plagiarized f(x)"

Examines claims related to f(x)'s "Rum Pum Pum Pum" (wisdom teeth wordplay) and "Pink Tape" album packaging/art film. Clarifies that the wordplay is common, the visual/lyrical references differ, MHJ wasn't responsible for song selection for f(x), and the packaging/art film comparisons are misleading when considering context and other examples in K-Pop.

"Belift Lab admitted they stole New Jeans identity"

Refutes the claim that Belift Lab admitted to plagiarism. Explains that Belift Lab's legal representation stated that *even if* MHJ's claims were true, she would still be guilty of defamation due to her knowledge of potential harm, focusing on damages rather than admitting the truth of the plagiarism claim itself.
